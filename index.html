Hannah Morin

Carnegie Mellon University · Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania · hmorin at andrew dot cmu dot edu

I am a third year Ph.D. student across the Engineering and Public Policy Department and the Materials Science and Engineering Department. I am advised by Prof. Jay Whitacre and Prof. Jeremy Michalek. My research lies at the intersection of battery technology and policy. Specifically I am interested in understanding how battery material and electrochemical properties constrain policy as well as how economic and human behavior constrains how we should design battery systems. For example, this includes working to understand the impact of fast charging on electric vehilce battery degradation. I approach my work with both experimental and computational modeling components including, accelerated aging studies,dynamic cycling, post-test characterization (SEM, TEM), techonoeconomic modeling, and uncertainty quantification with distributional and sensitivity analyses. I love talking about decarbonization more broadly an am interested in expanding my work into electrical grid and direct air capture areas.
